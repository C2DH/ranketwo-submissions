Scene 1: Attic Discovery (After the death of her grandmother, Lea discovers in the attic of the defunt’s house a suitcase covered by dust. Lea enters the dusty attic, curious and emotional)
Lea: (softly, to herself) Grandma, what secrets have you left behind?
(She finds a dusty suitcase and opens it, revealing books, letters, postcards, tapes, videotapes, floppy disks, and a vintage Walkman.)
Lea: (amazed) Oh this is… incredible.
________________


Scene 2: Initial Inventory
Lea (voice-over): No time to explore everything now, no car to carry this home, and no space in my tiny studio… but these memories deserve to be preserved. Okay, let's take some pictures and list what's in here.

(Lea carefully takes photos of each object, listing them on her smartphone.)
(Visuals: smartphone photos, notes appearing on screen: books, tapes, floppy disks, videotapes.)
________________


Scene 3: Technical Challenges 
Lea (calling her tech-savy friend Ada on the phone): Hey Ada, found some old tapes and floppy disks. Can you help?
Ada : Sure! I would need the technical specifications of your items to confirm that I can work with them.

(Lea measures the items, takes notes, and texts detailed technical specifications to her tech-savvy friend, Ada.)

Lea (calling Ada on the phone): Sending the specs now !
Ada (still on the phone, on speaker, but reviewing the text message she just received): I'm not equipped for the videotapes, but bring me samples of tapes and floppy disks when you come back!
________________


Scene 4: Cataloguing the Discoveries (Lea recording her voice with her smartphone, carefully describing each item aloud.)
Lea: Author, title (describing a book)….sender, receiver (describing a postcard)… date, location (describing a photo)... every detail matters !
(Lea picks up tapes, examining stickers.)
Lea: Stickers on these tapes say… "Family gathering, Christmas '83." To do: find tape player, listen, describe content, digitize…
________________


Scene 5: Analyzing Floppy Disks (with Ada) (Lea and Ada working together at Ada’s place. Ada examining a floppy disk.)
Ada: Bad news—the file's format is obsolete. Good news—we can still read its metadata.
Lea: Metadata?
Ada: Yep! File size, creation date, file type—still valuable clues!
________________


Scene 6: Time passed by and Lea has made an inventory of all the objects, she documented them and found missing information by talking to family members and friends and neighbours of her grandmother (Lea, thoughtfully studying a spreadsheet with metadata on her computer)
Lea (voice-over): Grandma was quiet, but her photos and recordings speak volumes. With the dates, places, and names I was able to trace, they now tell a fuller story, not just of our family, but of how our town once lived, and how it evolved. I'd love to contribute Grandma's photos to the local histories project. I need to keep them alive and make them accessible to anyone interested. But before that, I need Aunt Maria’s consent : she appears in many of them, and we're the only family members left.
(Lea calls an older family member.)
Lea: Hello? Aunt Maria? Would you agree to share these pictures online?
(Visual: consent form filled, family member's approval.)
________________


Closing Scene: (Screen fades to the project’s website displaying Grandma’s photographs.)
Lea (voice-over): Preserving memories isn't just about keeping the past alive. It's about sharing with purpose, respecting privacy, and ensuring our digital heritage is used ethically.
